Associated Tracks

Academic Track

The Academic Track is a Senior High School program designed to equip students with strong academic foundations for college and university education. It emphasizes critical thinking, research, and subject specialization across four strands: STEM, ABM, HUMSS, and GAS. This track is best suited for learners who plan to pursue higher education and professional careers such as engineering, business, medicine, law, education, and the social sciences.

What You Will Study?

▶
  • Language and Communication: Develop advanced reading, writing, speaking, and comprehension skills essential for academic and professional success.
  • Mathematics: Study mathematical principles that enhance logical reasoning, analytical thinking, and problem-solving abilities.
  • Science: Explore physical, biological, and earth sciences to understand scientific concepts, experimentation, and real-world applications.
  • Research: Learn proper research methods, including data collection, analysis, interpretation, and academic writing.
  • Social Sciences: Examine society, culture, governance, and human behavior to better understand social issues and institutions.
  • Humanities: Study philosophy, literature, ethics, and the arts to develop critical, creative, and reflective thinking.
  • Critical Thinking: Analyze problems, evaluate evidence, and form logical conclusions across different subjects.
  • Personal Development: Build self-awareness, emotional intelligence, goal-setting skills, and personal responsibility.
  • Health and Physical Education: Promote physical fitness, mental wellness, and healthy lifestyle choices.
  • Life and Career Preparation: Gain essential skills for college readiness, career planning, and lifelong learning.

Key Skills You Will Develop

▶
  • Critical Thinking: Ability to assess information, question assumptions, and make informed decisions.
  • Research & Analysis: Strengthen skills in data gathering, interpretation, and evidence-based reasoning.
  • Communication Skills: Improve written, oral, and presentation skills through essays, reports, and discussions.
  • Problem-Solving: Apply logical and systematic approaches to academic and real-world challenges.
  • Adaptability: Adjust effectively to different subjects, learning styles, and academic demands.
  • Time Management: Learn to prioritize tasks, manage deadlines, and balance academic responsibilities.
  • Interdisciplinary Thinking: Connect ideas across subjects to develop a broader and deeper understanding.
  • Independent Learning: Build discipline, initiative, and responsibility in completing academic tasks.
  • Analytical Writing: Express ideas clearly, logically, and persuasively through structured writing.
  • College Readiness: Develop study habits, discipline, and academic confidence needed for higher education.

Jobs And Careers

▶

Criminologist: Examines crime patterns, criminal behavior, and social factors to help improve law enforcement, crime prevention, and justice systems.

Journalism: Involves researching, writing, and reporting accurate information through print, broadcast, and digital media.

Communication: Focuses on delivering clear and effective messages through media, public speaking, advertising, and organizational communication.

Teaching: Educators guide learners by sharing knowledge, developing skills, and shaping values and critical thinking.

Psychology: Studies human behavior and mental processes, applied in counseling, education, research, and workplace settings.

Law: Legal professionals interpret laws, protect rights, and represent individuals or organizations in legal matters.

Public Relations: Manages public image and communication between organizations and their audiences.

Engineer: Designs and develops structures, systems, and technologies that address real-world problems.

Data Scientist: Analyzes complex data to identify patterns and support evidence-based decisions.

Research Scientist: Conducts experiments and studies to advance scientific knowledge and innovation.

Software Developer: Builds and maintains software applications using programming and problem-solving skills.

Architect: Designs functional, safe, and visually appealing buildings and structures.

Environmental Scientist: Studies environmental issues and develops solutions for sustainability.
