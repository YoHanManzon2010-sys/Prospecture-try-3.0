Associated Strands and Career

SCIENCE TECHNOLOGY ENGINEERING MATHEMATICS (STEM)

This strand focuses on subjects like mathematics, physics, chemistry, and biology, which are foundational for careers in science and technology.

What You Will Study?

▶
  • Science: Biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental science.
  • Technology: Information technology, computer science, and digital literacy.
  • Engineering: Principles of engineering, design thinking, and problem-solving.
  • Mathematics: Advanced mathematics, statistics, and data analysis.
  • Research: Subjects focused on scientific inquiry, data analysis, and logical reasoning.

Key Skills You Will Develop

▶
  • Problem-solving: Students learn to approach complex issues methodically and develop effective solutions.
  • Critical Thinking: Students learn to analyze information and different perspectives on human behavior and society.
  • Logical Reasoning & Analysis: This skill involves thinking clearly and systematically to solve problems. It means analyzing information, identifying patterns, and making sound conclusions based on facts rather than guesses or emotions.
  • Data Interpretation: Students learn to analyze and interpret data sets to draw meaningful conclusions.
  • Applying Scientific Method: Using a systematic process of questioning, experimenting, and analyzing results to reach evidence-based conclusions.

Jobs And Careers

▶

Doctor (Physician): Doctors diagnose, treat, and prevent illnesses using scientific knowledge and advanced medical technology to help patients maintain and improve their health.

Engineer (Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, etc.): Engineers apply scientific and mathematical principles to design, build, and maintain structures, machines, and systems that solve real-world problems.

Data Scientist: Data scientists analyze and interpret complex datasets using statistics, programming, and machine learning to support data-driven decision-making.

Research Scientist: Research scientists conduct experiments and studies to expand scientific knowledge and contribute to technological and medical advancements.

Software Developer: Software developers design, code, test, and maintain software applications and systems using programming languages and development tools.

Architect: Architects design safe, functional, and visually appealing buildings by combining engineering principles with creativity and technical skills.

Environmental Scientist: Environmental scientists study natural systems and develop solutions to environmental problems using scientific research and analysis.
