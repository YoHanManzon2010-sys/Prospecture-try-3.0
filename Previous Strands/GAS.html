Associated Strands and Career

General Academic Strand (GAS)

GAS stands for General Academic Strand, which is a flexible educational track in senior high school. It covers a variety of subjects, including humanities, social sciences, and management, preparing students for diverse fields in higher education. This strand allows students to explore different disciplines, making it suitable for those who are undecided about their specific career path.

What You Will Study?

▶
  • Humanities: Introduction to Philosophy, 21st Century Literature, World Religions.
  • Social Sciences: Understanding Culture, Society & Politics, Social Sciences, Applied Economics.
  • Management & Business: Organization & Management, Entrepreneurship.
  • Sciences: Earth & Life Science, Physical Science, General Mathematics, Statistics & Probability.
  • Communication & Tech: Oral Communication, Reading & Writing, Empowerment Technologies, Media & Information Literacy.
  • Practical Skills:Disaster Readiness & Risk Reduction, Research in Daily Life, Physical Education & Health.

Key Skills You Will Develop

▶
  • Critical Thinking & Problem Solving: Analyzing diverse subjects strengthens your ability to think logically and find solutions.
  • Communication: Oral and written skills are honed through subjects like Oral Communication and creative writing.
  • Research & Analysis: Exposure to social sciences and humanities builds strong research capabilities.
  • Adaptability: Since GAS covers various fields (Humanities, Social Sciences, Business), you become flexible and can pivot to different college majors.
  • Interdisciplinary Thinking:You learn to connect concepts from different areas, fostering a broad understanding.

Jobs And Careers

▶

Business & Management:Business and Management is the field focused on overseeing and coordinating an organization's resources and operations (like finance, HR, marketing) to achieve goals through strategic planning, leadership, and efficient execution, encompassing functions from daily administration to big-picture strategy for growth, sustainability, and competitiveness.

Humanities & Social Sciences: Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) are broad academic fields studying human culture, society, behavior, and expression, with Social Sciences using empirical methods (like sociology, psychology, economics) to analyze structures, while Humanities focus on qualitative, interpretive analysis (like philosophy, literature, history, arts) to understand human experience, culture, and meaning.

Education: Education is the process of acquiring knowledge, skills, values, and character through learning, encompassing formal schooling (kindergarten to university) and informal life experiences, aiming to develop potential, foster critical thinking, and prepare individuals for active, productive lives, contributing to personal growth, social stability, and economic development.

Government/Civil Service: Government/Civil Service involves working in public administration, policy-making, and delivering government services to citizens. It includes roles in local and national government agencies, regulatory bodies, and public institutions.
