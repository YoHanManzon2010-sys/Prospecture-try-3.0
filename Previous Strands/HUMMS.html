Associated Strands and Career

Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)

This strand focuses on subjects like literature, communication, and social sciences, which are foundational for language-based careers.

What You Will Study?

▶
  • Humanities: Literature, languages, philosophy, and religious studies.
  • Social Sciences: Psychology, sociology, political science, economics and communication.
  • Applied Skills: Creative writing, critical thinking, and community engagement.

Key Skills You Will Develop

▶
  • Communication Skills: Reading, writing, and public speaking are honed through various tasks and projects.
  • Critical Thinking: Students learn to analyze information and different perspectives on human behavior and society.
  • Empathy and Social Awareness: The strand fosters an understanding of diverse cultures and societal challenges.
  • Research Skills: Students develop the ability to conduct inquiries into social phenomena.

Jobs And Careers

▶

Criminologist: Studies the nature and causes of crime by analyzing crime patterns, criminal behavior, and social factors to help improve crime prevention and justice systems.

Journalism: Involves researching, writing, and reporting news and information through print, broadcast, and digital media to inform the public.

Communication: Professionals in this field convey information clearly and effectively through media, public speaking, advertising, and organizational communication.

Teaching: Educators play a crucial role in shaping learners by delivering knowledge, developing critical thinking, and guiding students academically and morally.

Psychology: Focuses on understanding human behavior and mental processes, with applications in counseling, education, research, and organizational settings.

Law: Legal professionals advocate justice, interpret laws, and represent individuals or organizations in legal matters and court proceedings.

Public Relations: Involves managing communication between organizations and the public to maintain a positive image and build strong relationships.
